F1 in Schools — STEM Lab
🌪️
Aerodynamics
F1 wings use Bernoulli's principle: faster airflow creates lower pressure above, generating downforce. At 250 km/h a car generates over 2,500 kg of downforce — 2.5× its own weight.L = ½ρv²CₗA → lift (or downforce)
🧱
Materials Science
Carbon fibre composites are 5× stronger than steel at ¼ the weight. F1 in Schools teams CNC-machine cars from aluminium billets or 3D-print in PETG, then sand and finish for minimum drag coefficient.σ = F/A → tensile stress (Pa)
📡
Data & Electronics
Real F1 cars stream 1,500+ sensor channels live. F1 in Schools uses photogate timing — measuring how long a car takes to break an infrared beam at a known position.v = d/t → speed from gate spacing
🔵
Physics of Motion
CO₂ cars accelerate from 0 to ~40 m/s in ~80ms, experiencing ~20G peak acceleration. F1 drivers feel 5G+ braking — more than many fighter pilots experience in combat.G = a/9.81 → a = Δv/Δt
